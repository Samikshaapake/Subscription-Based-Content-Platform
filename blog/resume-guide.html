Resume Writing Guide

Create a professional resume that stands out to recruiters

Introduction

A well-crafted resume is your first step toward landing your dream job. It should clearly highlight your skills, experience, and achievements while being concise and easy to read. This guide provides actionable tips to build an ATS-friendly, recruiter-approved resume.

Resume Structure

  • Header: Full name, contact information, LinkedIn/GitHub links.
  • Professional Summary: 2-3 lines summarizing your skills, experience, and career goals.
  • Education: Degrees, universities, graduation dates, and GPA if strong.
  • Skills: List relevant technical and soft skills (e.g., programming languages, tools).
  • Experience: Previous jobs, internships, or projects. Use bullet points with achievements and measurable results.
  • Certifications & Achievements: Relevant certificates, awards, hackathon wins.
  • Optional: Projects, volunteer work, or extracurricular activities.

Resume Writing Tips

  • Keep your resume **1 page** for students or 1–2 pages for experienced candidates.
  • Use **action verbs** like Developed, Designed, Implemented, Optimized.
  • Quantify achievements: “Increased website traffic by 30% in 3 months.”
  • Keep formatting **clean and consistent**: uniform fonts, bullet points, spacing.
  • Tailor your resume to the **job description**: highlight skills and experience relevant to the role.
  • Use **keywords** from the job posting to make it ATS-friendly.
  • Proofread multiple times to avoid typos or grammatical errors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Writing long paragraphs instead of concise bullet points.
  • Listing irrelevant skills or experience.
  • Using generic or vague descriptions: "Worked on multiple projects."
  • Poor formatting, inconsistent font sizes, or cluttered layout.
  • Neglecting to include measurable achievements.
