Top Interview Tips

Prepare effectively for coding, technical, and HR interviews

Introduction

Cracking interviews at top tech companies requires technical skills, problem-solving abilities, and strong communication. This guide provides a step-by-step approach for coding, system design, and behavioral rounds, along with practical strategies and resources to maximize your chances of success.

Coding Interview Tips

  • Practice problems regularly on platforms like LeetCode, HackerRank, and Codeforces.
  • Focus on Data Structures: Arrays, Strings, Linked Lists, Stacks, Queues, Trees, Graphs, Hash Tables.
  • Master Algorithms: Sorting, Searching, Recursion, Dynamic Programming, Backtracking, BFS/DFS, Greedy, Divide & Conquer.
  • Learn problem patterns: Sliding Window, Two Pointers, Hashing, Fast & Slow pointers.
  • Always clarify the problem before coding: Ask about edge cases and constraints.
  • Write clean, readable code with meaningful variable names.
  • Optimize solutions for time and space complexity; mention trade-offs during interviews.
  • Practice explaining your thought process aloud to simulate real interviews.

System Design Interview Tips

  • Understand basics: Scalability, Load Balancing, Caching, Databases (SQL/NoSQL), API design.
  • Prepare common systems: URL shortener, social media feed, chat system, e-commerce backend.
  • Draw diagrams: Use boxes and arrows to explain components clearly.
  • Think about trade-offs: Performance, reliability, cost, maintainability.
  • Discuss alternatives and explain reasoning for choosing a design.

Behavioral / HR Interview Tips

  • Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to answer behavioral questions.
  • Be prepared for questions like: “Tell me about yourself”, “Why this company?”, “Describe a challenge you overcame”.
  • Demonstrate teamwork, leadership, and problem-solving through examples.
  • Show adaptability and a growth mindset – companies value learning agility.
  • Maintain confident body language, clear speech, and professional demeanor.

Mock Interviews & Practice

  • Schedule mock interviews with peers or mentors to simulate real interview conditions.
  • Use platforms like interviewing.io for realistic interview practice.
  • Review previous interview experiences on Glassdoor and Blind.
  • Track your progress and revisit weak areas regularly for continuous improvement.

Suggested Daily Preparation Plan

  • 1–2 hours: Solve coding problems and practice algorithms.
  • 30 minutes: Revise system design concepts and past questions.
  • 30 minutes: Behavioral/HR practice (mock interviews, STAR method answers).
  • Optional: 15–30 minutes reading tech blogs or company updates to stay informed.
  • Weekly: Take a full mock interview covering coding, system design, and behavioral rounds.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not practicing coding problems consistently.
  • Ignoring time and space complexity in solutions.
  • Rushing into code without clarifying requirements.
  • Providing vague or generic answers in behavioral interviews.
  • Neglecting research about the company and its culture.
  • Failing to review previous mistakes and learn from them.
