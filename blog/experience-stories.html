Candidate Experiences

Learn from real-life experiences of professionals and students

Introduction

Understanding real candidate experiences helps you prepare better and avoid common pitfalls. In this blog, we share stories from students and professionals who have successfully cracked interviews at top companies, along with the lessons they learned.

Success Stories

  • Google Intern: A student practiced LeetCode problems daily for 3 months, focused on data structures, and participated in mock interviews with peers. This preparation helped them clear both coding and behavioral rounds.
  • Amazon SDE: Candidate emphasized system design preparation and learned cloud concepts. Detailed resume showcasing projects impressed the recruiter.
  • Microsoft Full-Time: Combined coding practice with behavioral storytelling. Used the STAR method to answer HR questions, sharing challenges and results.

Challenges & Lessons

  • Many candidates struggled initially with time management during coding rounds; structured daily practice helped overcome this.
  • Some overlooked behavioral questions; practicing STAR-based answers improved confidence and clarity.
  • System design questions were challenging for freshers; creating diagrams and understanding trade-offs made a big difference.
  • Networking and learning from others’ experiences helped candidates understand company culture and expectations.

Tips from Candidates

  • Start preparation early and maintain a consistent practice schedule.
  • Focus on problem-solving skills rather than memorizing solutions.
  • Take mock interviews seriously to simulate real scenarios.
  • Review past experiences and failures to identify weak areas.
  • Research the company’s culture, values, and recent news before interviews.
